10 Packing Hacks for Long Trips That Will Save You Money and Stress

Sarah Jenkins

Senior Travel Editor

"The world is a book and those who do not travel read only one page. But those who pack poorly read that page very uncomfortably."

Packing for a long trip can be a daunting task. Whether you're embarking on a month-long backpacking adventure across Europe or a multi-city business trip in Asia, the struggle to fit everything you need into a suitcase while adhering to airline weight limits is real. Over-packing leads to heavy luggage fees and sore backs, while under-packing can leave you scrambling for essentials in a foreign country.

After visiting over 40 countries and living out of a suitcase for months at a time, I've refined my packing strategy down to a science. Here are my top 10 packing hacks that will change the way you travel forever.

1. Roll, Don't Fold

This is the oldest trick in the book for a reason. Rolling your clothes instead of folding them not only saves a significant amount of space but also minimizes wrinkles. For even more space-saving, try the "Ranger Roll" technique used by the military to keep your socks, underwear, and t-shirts in tight, compact bundles that won't unravel in your bag.

2. Use Packing Cubes

If you haven't jumped on the packing cube bandwagon yet, now is the time. These zippered fabric containers help you organize your luggage by category (shirts in one, pants in another). Not only do they compress your clothing to maximize space, but they also make unpacking a breeze. When you arrive at your hotel, simply lift the cubes out of your suitcase and into the drawers—done in seconds!

3. The 5-4-3-2-1 Rule

For a week-long trip, try this simple formula to ensure you don't overpack:

  • 5 sets of socks and underwear
  • 4 tops
  • 3 bottoms
  • 2 pairs of shoes
  • 1 hat

Adjust the numbers based on your trip length, but the principle remains: limit your items and plan for laundry if you're traveling longer than a week.

4. Wear Your Bulkiest Items

Winter coats and hiking boots take up massive amounts of precious suitcase real estate. Always wear your heaviest shoes and thickest jacket on the plane. You can always take the jacket off once you're on board and use it as a pillow. This simple trick can free up 20-30% of your luggage space.

5. Solid Toiletries are a Game Changer

Liquids are heavy, prone to spilling, and subject to strict airport security regulations. Switch to solid shampoo bars, conditioner bars, solid perfume, and even toothpaste tabs. They last longer, take up less space, and you'll breeze through TSA without having to pull out that little plastic bag.

Pro Tip:

Place a dryer sheet in your suitcase to keep your clothes smelling fresh throughout your trip, especially if you're mixing clean and dirty laundry.

6. Stuff Your Shoes

Don't let the space inside your shoes go to waste. Stuff them with socks, chargers, or other small items. This not only uses dead space but also helps your shoes maintain their shape during transit. Wrap shoes in shower caps (often free at hotels) to keep the soles from dirtying your clothes.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of packing takes practice, but these hacks are a great place to start. Remember, the goal of travel is to experience the world, not to worry about your luggage. Pack light, pack smart, and you'll find yourself moving through airports and train stations with ease, ready for whatever adventure awaits.

Happy travels!
